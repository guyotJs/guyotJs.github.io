Cutlerties

All Things Common or The Order of Enoch  


    This principle seems to be accepted or believed in by quite a few of the believers in the Restoration movement, but for various reasons not practiced.  It seems to be a condition or a way of life that can be achieved only by a people with a sufficient portion of the Lord's Holy Spirit to enable them to work together in love and union.   A people with only the natural mind of man to guide them will never be able to achieve this state.  This principle calls for a people who will give unto their God their all, believing they are called to do so.  The Lord has said in various places and at various times, that "if you are not one you are not mine".  If this is true, then man with only the natural mind cannot be one, and will fail to attain this goal.  A sacrifice of all earthly things is required, and as the Lectures on Faith brings out, "any religion that does not require man to come to these principles will never give man the power to gain the faith necessary to lay hold upon eternal life.   They also teach we cannot expect to gain our salvation in the Eternal World and associate with the former saints who gave their all and gained their salvation unless we, in like manner, make the same offerings and sacrifices.

     Believing God is unchangeable, a just and a righteous God, could we have full faith in him were He to allow one privileges above another?  We are taught He is no respecter of persons but He is of conduct.  In all ages of the world when there has been a great outpouring of the Spirit, what has it led people to?  God and all His Holy Prophets have always taught the principles of Equality.  How does a man achieve this Equality?  Does he do it by giving a tenth of what he earns?  Will that produce a condition where one man has no possessions above another?  Will it lead to men working together in love and union?  It seems that man has always been reluctant to  accept the principles taught in the Holy Scriptures.   Often they think they have a better plan than the Lord's,  but following their plans they always fail.  First, it takes a certain portion of the Lord's Spirit to create in man the desire for that condition, and then additional Spirit to meet the obstacles met in reaching that goal.  When sufficient Spirit is secured to do this, then what does it lead men to do?  It leads them to work in love and union,  and as stated in the Book of Mormon regarding the 200 years they lived to this, there were no happier people on the face of the earth.  

     The Lord created man and put him here on earth with the need of food, clothing, and these material things, and He has said that there is enough given for all, were it (is) used as He intended.   Whenever man has met these conditions and made these sacrifices,  inevitably the blessings of Heaven are granted and man has received far more than he ever could following his own natural desires.  The principle of all things common, if adhered to, will give unto man the things the Lord would want him to have, and will achieve a condition such as Enoch's people who became so righteous they were taken from the earth as the world was not worthy of them.  The Lord cares nothing for these material things;   He simply wants the testimony of man that man puts God ahead;  thus when He required these blood offerings etc., He wanted the firstlings of their flocks, not the culls they were going to throw out anyway.  Can we quarrel with this principle?   Can we truly say that this is not the best way to go?  Then if we believe in it, why don't we keep it?  Why not make every effort to live to it and receive the attendant blessings?  Wouldn't it be worth the sacrifice to enjoy these blessings, and then when we go before our maker for our final judgment,  have Him say, "Enter into my rest at the right hand of the Father"?
