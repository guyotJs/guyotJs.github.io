Cutlerite

 Reorganization Of The Church Of Jesus Christ

Fisher’s Grove
Fremont County, Iowa
September 19, 1853

The people of Fisher’s Grove assembled at the house of Brother Edmund Fisher, and after some instruction from (Father) Alpheus Cutler proceeded to reorganize The Church Of Jesus Christ as far as circumstances and wisdom would dictate, as the Church that was organized by the Prophet Joseph Smith had through transgression become a rejected people as pertaining to the Church. It therefore became necessary that something should be done or that the door should be opened, as God always provides a way that the honest in heart may have a chance for salvation. In this case he left means whereby the Church and Kingdom might again be reorganized in it’s purity, that the sinner might repent and (being willing to follow the example set by our Savior) go forward and be baptized for a remission of their sins, making a covenant at the water's edge to keep all the commandments of God that had been or might be given while we live, and taking Joseph Smith the Prophet of God, The Bible, The Book Of Mormon, The Book Of Doctrine and Covenants and the Constitution of these United States as a platform for our faith and doctrine. With this view they proceeded to reorganize the Church as follows: Firstly, Alpheus Cutler chosen as our "head" or "chief councilor" and sustained by a unanimous vote; Secondly, Brother Edmund Fisher chosen and sustained as President of The Church Of Jesus Christ by a clear vote; Thirdly, Chauncey Whiting chosen and sustained as Clerk of The Church Of Jesus Christ by a clear vote.

The meeting was then closed by prayer.

The preceding account was taken from the minutes of the meeting as recorded in the records of The Church Of Jesus Christ (Cutlerites). Record book #1 – page 1.
