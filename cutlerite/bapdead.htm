Baptism for the Dead

     The ordinance of baptism for the dead was performed in Paul's Day. In writing his first epistle to the Corinthians, talking about the resurrection and trying to show those people that there was to be a resurrection for all mankind, he asked them the question, "Else what shall they do which are baptized for the dead, if the dead rise not at all? Why are they then baptized for the dead?". (I Corinthians 15:29, Inspired Version.)

     When God gave the commandment in 1841 that the Nauvoo temple was to be built, he also granted a sufficient length of time for the accomplishment of this work.

     "But, behold, at the end of this appointment, your baptisms for your dead shall not be acceptable unto me; and if you do not these things at the end of the appointment, ye shall be rejected as a church with your dead, saith the Lord your God." (Doctrine and Covenants, section 107,verse 11a, RLDS)

     The temple was to be similar to the temple in Kirtland, Ohio, though larger in dimension, and provision was to be made for a baptismal font where the lately revealed ordinance of the baptism for the dead could be performed.

     When the appointed time expired the temple still lacked much of being finished, ready for dedication to the Lord, so the rejection promised by the Lord became a fact.

     The confusion between the Restoration churches exists today because most of the churches do not believe of this rejection, but chose their own leader and continued on with the original church after collecting as many as possible of the historical records of the church, journals of the elders, minutes of the conferences and council meetings etc.  Soon they were busily engaged in changing, revising and editing all that came into their hands. In many cases the records were deliberately altered to conform to new doctrines and practices not taught in the church before, while others were suppressed or destroyed. When the church was dissolved due to transgression, the authority or priesthood rights were preserved in the Kingdom Order.
