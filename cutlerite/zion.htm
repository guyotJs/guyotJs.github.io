About Zion

     This is to tell you about an experience that came to me in the early 1950's.

     It was on a Sunday morning when the Spirit came to me and it stayed with me all day.  I had been visited by the Spirit many times before, but at this time I was given a look into the future of Zion.  It was not like an audible voice speaking, but as we are told when we are confirmed a member in the church, "a still small voice".

     Our people had been told by a former leader in the church, that when we received a revelation from God, it would cite us back to the written word, or the scriptures.  There were many scriptures brought to my mind to confirm the message as being authentic.  There were too many references given to relate them all but I will try to write some of them,   hoping it will be of interest to many believers in the Restoration.

      I had prayed much in times past to know how Zion would be brought about, for I saw all around me the disunion among the believers in the Restoration Movement.  Now this was the exact opposite of the ionic plan held forth in the scriptures, for the plan to build Zion is based on complete unity among its members.  For it is written, "If you are not one, you are not mine".  How could this be brought about?  I had never doubted from the very first that such a plan would be put into effect for the scriptures plainly tell us that God has had people on this earth in former days who, through the outpouring of His spirit were able to live above sin.

     This is the requirement of God's law, that we come to the point of perfection, for Jesus said, "Ye are therefore commanded to be prefect, even as your Father who is in Heaven is perfect".  Nephi tell us in the Book of Mormon, that God never gives a commandment to the children of men, except He opens the way for it to be kept.  This leaves men without excuse for disobeying the laws that would bring them to perfection.

     But how are men to know what God's law really is amid all the confusion we find about us today?   In the very beginning God gave His law through Adam,  with the admonition that he teach it freely to his children.  This same law given to Adam, brought about a Zion in Enoch's day, and Adam lived in the days of Enoch.  One only needs to read the story of Enoch and his followers to realize what a terrific change would take place were the same principles adhered to in our time.  The laws of God as given to Moses would have had the same effect with the Children of Israel, had they lived them,  but as a people they chose to become a law unto themselves, and after traveling for forty years, their carcasses fell in the wilderness.

     These things should be an example to us; the obedience and the disobedience to the laws of God.   Lehi and Nephi brought the laws of Moses with them from Jerusalem.  They were written on the plates of brass, and it was under these laws that the Nephites lived until Christ appeared to them on this land after His resurrection.  It is evident that by living to the principles set forth in this law, the Nephites were able to prepare themselves to stand in the very presence of their risen Saviour.

    We are told that only the more righteous of the Nephites were spared from destruction during the great three day storm that swept the wicked from the face of this land at the time of the crucifixion of Christ.  While He was with them, Christ expounded all their scriptures and through their keeping of these laws, a Zion was enjoyed for many years, and not one soul was lost.  The meaning of the word "restoration" is to have a Zion again upon the earth as in former days.

     Now, the things that were shown me while in the spirit on that Sunday morning was, that God would work along that same plan in starting this last part of His work, as He did in Joseph Smith's day when He revealed to him where the plates were hidden.  When Joseph received the gold plates from the hands of the angel, he was instructed to translate only that part of the plates which were not sealed.  Now the mere fact that those gold plates had come into Joseph's possession was not enough to establish him as a prophet and seer in the eyes of others, so for this reason God required that other men be called as witnesses of the truth of Joseph's claims, regarding the gold plates.  Because of these instructions we find the testimony of the eleven witnesses printed in the front of every Book of Mormon that has ever been published.  Three of these men saw the angel come down from Heaven and lay the plates before them.  The other eight witnesses saw only the plates, but they were all commanded to bear witness to the whole world, to the truth of what they had been privileged to behold.  It is a matter of record that not one of these witnesses ever denied their testimony during their lifetime.

     This same plan will be used when God sees fit, in His wisdom, to raise up another seer and bring forth the sealed portion of the gold plates which have been kept back because of the wickedness of the people.  Joseph Smith was told, "Touch not the things which are sealed, for I will bring them forth in my own due time: for I will shew unto the children of men, that I am able to do mine own work.  Wherefore, when thou hast read the words which I have commanded thee, and have obtained the witnesses which I have promised unto thee, then shalt thou seal up the book again, and hide it up unto me, that I may preserve the works which thou hast not read until I shall see fit in mine own wisdom, to reveal all things unto the children of men". Page 151, RLDS edition and page 97, LDS edition of the Book of Mormon.  And again "For the book shall be sealed by the power of God, and the revelation which was sealed, shall be kept in the book until the own due time of the Lord, that they may come forth: for, behold, they reveal all things from the foundation of the world unto the end thereof.  And the day cometh that the words of the book which were sealed, shall be read upon the housetops, and they shall be read by the power of Christ; And all things shall be revealed unto the children of men which ever have been among the children of men, and which ever will be, even unto the end of the earth".  Page 149, RLDS edition, page 96, LDS edition, Book of Mormon.

     This will be the work of the second seer, when he becomes qualified in the eyes of God, and proves by his works, that he is worthy to receive and translate that portion of the plates.   The fact that he has the plates will be ample proof that he has the Melchisedec Priesthood handed down from Joseph Smith's day, for God will not work through any other channel than that which He has already established.

     This prophet will also have the gift of revelation to instruct him in all things.  He will call in men of good character from the other churches of the Restoration to bear witness to the truth of these claims, which will convert them, and they in turn will be instruments in the hands of God in bringing others to the knowledge of the truth through their testimony.

     To build a Zion, God must overcome every obstacle that confronts mankind today.  The plan which God gave Enoch, brought his people to perfection, and the same plan will establish Zion in the last days.  The plan of equality will do away with poverty, the housing difficulties, and the racial differences.  The honesty which will prevail among a converted people, will solve the greater part of our social and economic problems.   These things cannot come to pass until the outpouring of the Holy Spirit is realized as it was in the days of Pentecost, as well as at the time of Christ's visit to this country.

     God has said that it would come to pass in His own due time.  Some are inclined to believe that man can bring about a Zion through keeping the commandments.  This is true in part, but God's purposes will never be accomplished until He gives His followers a double portion of Spirit.  This is a matter of record.  Before this takes place we must all be born of the Spirit, not of many spirits, but of one, and through equal sacrifice we will receive an equal portion of spirit.  Thus God will unite His church.

     God's plan is based on time, and He is still on schedule.  He raised up Joseph Smith at the appointed time, as He did all the former prophets, and He will make known the next prophet and seer at the proper time, for He knows who and where he is. "For God's arm is not shortened, neither is His strength abated".
