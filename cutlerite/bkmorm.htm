Book of Mormon

     The Book of Mormon is an abridgment of the record of the people of Nephi and also of the Lamanites and Jaredites, written by way of commandment, and also by the spirit of prophecy and of revelation.  It was written and sealed up and hid unto the Lord, that they might not be destroyed; to come forth by the gift and power of God unto the interpretation there of; sealed by the hand of Moroni, to come forth in due time by the way of Gentile; the interpretation there of by the gift of God.

     The testimony of three witnesses, viz. Oliver Cowdery, David Whitmer and Martin Harris testified they had seen the engravings which are upon the plates and had been shown unto them by the power of God and not of man and the Lord commanded them to bear record of it that they had been translated by the gift and power of God.  In addition to the three witnesses, eight other men were called to be witnesses and each gave their testimony after the plates had been shown them by Joseph Smith Jr. and gave their name unto the world that which they had seen.

                              1.   Christian Whitmer
                              2.   Jacob Whitmer
                              3.   Peter Whitmer Jr.
                              4.   John Whitmer
                              5.   Hiram Page
                              6.   Joseph Smith Sr.
                              7.   Hyram Smith
                              8.   Samuel H. Smith

     None of the witnesses ever changed their testimony during their lifetime.
