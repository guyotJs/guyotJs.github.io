The Church of Jesus Christ
Cutlerites

 Polygamy Condemned

     As early as 1835 the Church of Jesus Christ had been accused of practicing polygamy.  A letter written by Joseph Smith while in jail in Liberty, Missouri on December 16, 1838 explains the Church's views on the subject.  It is printed in the book 'Alpheus Cutler and the Church of Jesus Christ' on pages 252 and 253.

     Hyram Smith's letter to a group of saints on China Creek, Hancock County, Illinois and published in the Times and Seasons, volume 5, page 474, under the date of March 15, 1844 only a few months before his death, claimed it was never a part of the restored gospel.

     An eminent jurist, Federal Judge John F. Phillips, reviewed the evidence presented by the Utah Mormon Church in legal proceedings wherein the origin of the doctrine and practice of polygamy was a vital issue, and he held that Brigham Young, and not Joseph Smith, was the author and instigator of that obnoxious and heretical doctrine.

     The first edition of the Doctrine and Covenants in which section 132 appears is 1876, printed by the Deseret News, Salt Lake City, Utah'32 years after the prophet's death.  The Cutlerite Church owns a Doctrine and Covenants printed by the Utah Church in 1851, and it did not contain section 132.

     The practice of polygamy has been prevalent since the dawn of time, but has always been condemned by God as being a sin.  His feelings on the matter are very plainly shown in all three of the books we use as scripture.  Following are just a few of the many references in which this is proven:

     In the Bible, it tells us in Genesis, chapter 2, verse 30, how marriage was initiated in the very beginning.  'Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife;  and they shall be one flesh.'  This concept is reiterated and more thoroughly described in Mark, chapter 10, verses 6 through 10.

     In First Corinthians, chapter 7, verses 2 and 3, it says:  'Nevertheless, I say, to avoid fornication, let every man have his own wife, and let every woman have her own husband.  Let the husband render unto the wife due benevolence;  and likewise also the wife unto the husband.'

     In Ephesians, chapter 5, verses 31 through 33, it says:  'For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall be joined unto his wife, and they two shall be one flesh.  This is a great mystery;  but I speak concerning Christ and the Church.  Nevertheless, let every one of you in particular so love his wife even as himself;  and the wife see that she reverence her husband.'  Please note that it says 'they two', not three, four or more.

     In the Book Of Mormon, I quote from the writings of Jacob, chapter 1, verse 15:  'And now it came to pass that the people of Nephi, under the reign of the second king, began to grow hard in their hearts, and indulge themselves somewhat in wicked practices, such as like unto David of old, desiring many wives and concubines, and also Solomon, his son.'

     In Jacob, chapter 2, verses 31 through 33, it says 'But the word of God burthens me because of your grosser crimes.  For behold, thus saith the Lord, this people begin to wax in iniquity;  they understand not the scriptures:  for they seek to excuse themselves in committing whoredoms, because of the things which were written concerning David, and Solomon his son.  Behold, David and Solomon truly had many wives, and concubines, which thing was abominable before me, saith the Lord.'

     In verses 36 and 37 it says 'Wherefore, my brethren, hear me, and hearken to the word of the Lord:  For there shall not any man among you have save it be one wife;  and concubines he shall have none:  For I, the Lord God, delighteth in the chastity of women.  And whoredoms are an abomination before me:  thus saith the Lord of hosts.'

     In the Doctrine and Covenants (1846 edition) I quote from section 13, verse 7:  'Thou shalt love thy wife with all thy heart, and shall cleave unto her and none else;  and he that looketh upon a woman to lust after her, shall deny the faith, and shall not have the spirit, and if he repents not he shall be cast out.'

     In section 35, verse 3, we read 'And again I say unto you, that whoso forbiddeth to marry, is not ordained of God, for marriage is ordained of God unto man:  wherefore it is lawful that he should have one wife, and they twain shall be one flesh, and all this that the earth might answer the end of it's creation;  and that it might be filled with the measure of man, according to his creation before the world was made.'

     Section 109, known as the Marriage Covenant, was not a revelation, but was read at the General Assembly of the Church at Kirtland on August 17, 1835 where the acceptance and adoption of the first edition of the Doctrine and Covenants was voted on and acknowledged by a unanimous vote.  This article was also read, voted on and acknowledged by a unanimous vote and was ordered to be printed in the book.  It is a clear affirmation of the laws of the Church concerning the sacrament of marriage.  You will notice that polygamy is condemned.  I quote from verse 4:  'Inasmuch as this Church of Christ has been reproached with the crime of fornication, and polygamy:  we declare that we believe, that one man should have one wife;  and one woman, but one husband, except in case of death when either is at liberty to marry again.'

    There is an excellent chapter on polygamy in the book 'Alpheus Cutler and the Church of Jesus Christ' by Rupert and Daisy Fletcher, which fully explains the Church's belief and feelings on the subject.  This chapter begins on page 247 and ends on page 258 of said book, and contains many fine references for the reader.

     There is also proof of Cutler's stand on the subject in the book 'Joseph Smith Fought Polygamy' by Richard and Pamela Price (See pages 203-204).  This is an excellent book, and is a must for anyone who is seriously researching the subject.  It is well written, and is completely backed up by references throughout.

     We believe that we serve an unchangeable God, as described in the third lecture of the 'Lectures On Faith', which were published and placed in the front of the first several editions of the Doctrine and Covenants.  These lectures were also voted on and accepted by a unanimous vote at the General Assembly previously mentioned, and were deemed judiciously arranged, compiled and profitable for doctrine.

     I quote from the third lecture, (1846 Doctrine and Covenants, page 44, verses 15 and 16) which is describing the character of God:  'Thirdly, That he changes not, neither is there variableness with him;  but that he is the same from everlasting to everlasting, being the same yesterday, today and forever;  and that his course is one eternal round, without variation.  Fourthly, That he is a God of truth and cannot lie.'

     If this is the case, then how can we accept a doctrine that teaches us that God can 'change the rules' from time to time according to his will?  If we are to believe that God truly cannot lie, and we say we accept the scriptures as gospel, then we have to accept the fact that anyone who defends the concept of plural marriage must surely be in error.
