Inspired Version of the Bible


     In addition to translating the Book of Mormon, Joseph Smith Jr. corrected many errors contained in the King James translation of the Bible. The corrected scripture is known as the Inspired Version of the Bible.  This version of the Holy Scriptures, containing the old and the new testaments, was revised through inspiration by Joseph Smith Jr., beginning June 1830 and was finished July 2, 1833.  The manuscripts, at his death in 1844, were left in the hands of his widow, Emma,  where they remained until the Spring of 1866, when they were delivered to a committee appointed to procure them for publication.

     It is declared in the Book of Mormon, 1st Book of Nephi, Chapter 3, verses 168-172, "For behold, they have taken away from the gospel of the lamb many parts which are plain and most precious, and also many covenants of the Lord have they taken away and all this have they done that they might pervert the right ways of the Lord;  that they might blind the eyes and harden the hearts of the children of men:  wherefore, thou seest that after the book hath gone forth through the hands of the great and abominable church that there are many plain and precious things taken away from the book, which is the book of the Lamb of God, and after these plain and precious things were taken away, it goeth forth unto all the nations of the Gentiles".

     It states in the book of Doctrine and Covenants, Section 42, verse 15, "Thou shalt ask, and my scriptures shall be given as I have appointed, and they shall be preserved in safety; and it is expedient that thou shouldst hold thy peace concerning them, and not teach them until thou hast received them in full.  And I give unto you a commandment, that then ye shall teach them unto all men; for they shall be taught unto all nations, kindreds, tongues, and people."
