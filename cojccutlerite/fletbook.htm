ABOUT THE BOOK
Published in1974

     From time to time individuals have requested historical data concerning Alpheus Cutler and the church he represented.  Many times the inquiries have gone unanswered because such information seemed not to be available.    Considerable research in recent years, however, has been successful to some degree, and this book has been prepared in the hope that it may prove helpful.

     An effort has been made to arrange in chronological order the known facts about Mr. Cutler, his family, places where he resided, and his affiliation with the Church of Jesus Christ and the restored gospel.  Often names, dates, and places are missing that would add much to the value of the story.  Hopefully some of these facts may yet be found through continued search.

     This book is dedicated to the young people of the church who are descendants of these early families, that they may more fully appreciate the legacy that is theirs.  The men and women who preserved the authority and the church for our generations are now gone, but their faith, diligence, and prayers have made possible the religious heritage we enjoy today.  Let us cherish the sacred gift and strive to so conduct our lives that its name may be honored and revered.

About The Authors

     Mr. and Mrs. Rupert J. Fletcher collaborated in writing an interesting book about one of the lesser known churches of the Restoration.  The historical sketch written by Mrs. Fletcher (Daisy Whiting Fletcher), and the articles on doctrinal beliefs by Mr. Fletcher, who at the time was president of the church, are combined to give the reader an account of The Church of Jesus Christ, its background, its teachings, and pattern for future development. 

     The Fletchers are both descended from early-day believers in the restored gospel.  They were born in Clitherall, Minnesota, and moved to Independence, Missouri, in 1928 and 1929, with their parents.  They were married in 1930 and resided in Independence until Mr. Fletchers death in 1974 and Mrs. Fletchers death in 1975.
