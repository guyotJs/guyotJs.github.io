     Preface:  This article was written for the express purpose of supplying information to those who, at various times, may request information on certain beliefs and doctrine of our church.  In the interest of brevity scriptural quotations will be held to a minimum and the article itself as brief as possible and still convey the information requested.  Further information concerning specific subjects can be furnished from various sources already written.

The Kingdom Authority-The Order of Seven

     This subject is relatively unknown to most of the restoration believers, but it is a subject of such prime importance that a knowledge of it should be acquired by all believers in this great latter day work.  Were it not for the "Order of Seven or Kingdom Authority" the church, following it's rejection at Nauvoo, Illinois would have disappeared from the earth and the gospel with it's attendant authority would have been lost to man.  Through the angelic ordinations by the seven heavenly messengers sent to convey this authority to Joseph Smith and his followers, people on the earth were again empowered or authorized of God to set up His church on the earth.  It was prophesied then that it was restored for the last time and would never again leave the earth until all things promised were accomplished.  Upon rejection of the church at the failure to build the Nauvoo Temple in the appointed time the church ceased to exist but the kingdom order or authority of the Holy Priesthood's continued with those holding these ordinations, and only through this kingdom order could the church have been reorganized and setup; the same authority by which it was established originally.

     This will seem a strange doctrine to many of the restoration believers.  Alpheus Cutler was number seven in that order at the time of the churches reorganization in 1853.  Prior to that time he had been ordained by the Prophet Joseph Smith to all the powers and authority necessary to carry forward the work that the Prophet had been commissioned to do but was unable to complete because of his death.  This was borne out by the testimony of several who were present at that time.  Mother (Lois) Cutler testified she was present when the above mentioned ordination was given and that she saw the Prophet lay his hands upon Cutler's head and ordain him to all the rights, keys, powers, privileges, and blessings belonging to that condition and whose right to act as prophet, seer, and revelator was to be in force upon the whole world from that very hour, thus fulfilling the law of succession as given by the Lord to the Prophet Joseph and the church, RLDS Section 43, paragraph 2.  Several others of the Manti group also testified that they were in attendance at the general conference in Nauvoo, Illinois when Joseph, in his address to the congregation informed them he had men prepared who could carry on the work as well as he, should he be taken;  that he then placed his hand upon Alpheus Cutler and said "And here is the man who will do it".  Marcus Shaw alludes to this when he speaks of the Lord setting up His kingdom in 1843, (Our Church record book number 2, section 1, page 239).  No record has been found as to the identity of the quorum of seven with the exception of the last two;  Cutler stated that Uncle John Smith was number six and he was number seven at this time.  Cutler waited until the sign was given he had been forewarned would come before setting up the church.  The new church required all former members to be re-baptized for it was a re-organization rather than a continuation.

     In section 83, RLDS Doctrine and Covenants it speaks of Joseph Smith and six elders who united their hearts and lifted their voices on high and were given instructions concerning the church and how to conduct it's affairs.

     In Section 49, RLDS book of Doctrine and Covenants, paragraph 2c, it speaks of "holy men that ye know not of",  so evidently there was a quorum or group that not everyone was aware of.  The Lord, being a God of order would surely not have deemed it necessary to send His holy angels to earth to convey unto men this authority to establish a church in 1830 unless this same authority was necessary and to be present at the following reorganization.  It is only by the authority of those priesthoods that man can represent God here on earth and as spoken of in Section 84, RLDS book of Doctrine and Covenants, "it must needs remain, through you and your lineage, until the restoration of all things spoken by the mouths of all the holy prophets since the world began, and through this Priesthood, a savior unto my people Israel".

     More details concerning this subject can be acquired by searching the revelations in the book of Doctrine and Covenants, as well as the Bible and the Book of Mormon; also by referring to the book, "Alpheus Cutler and The Church of Jesus Christ", written by Brother Rupert Fletcher.  Brother Fletcher wrote in considerable detail on this and other subjects and his book is highly recommended reading for anyone desirous of learning the beliefs and doctrine of The Church of Jesus Christ.   Reference Material.
