Doctrine & Covenants (1846 edition)

     This is a reprint of the Fourth American Edition of the Doctrine & Covenants.  The first edition was printed in 1835, and contained the revelations given to Joseph Smith prior to that date.  The second edition was printed in 1844, and contained all of the revelations that were included in the 1835 edition plus seven more that he received between 1835 and his death in 1844.  The third edition, published in 1845, and the fourth edition, published in 1846, were both reprints only of the 1844 edition.  The Church of Jesus Christ (Cutlerite) has adopted this 1846 edition as it's standard because of it's availability.  We believe that Joseph Smith was the lawgiver for these last days, just as Moses was for his time, and Christ was for his.  Therefore, we accept this edition in it's purity, with no additions and no deletions from the original.  Other factions have seen fit to strip the Lectures On Faith from the beginning of the book, and add or delete other revelations at will.  We try to adhere to the original doctrine and laws that were given to Joseph when God's true Authority was once again restored to the earth.

Alpheus Cutler and The Church Of Jesus Christ

     Mr. and Mrs. Rupert J. Fletcher have collaborated in writing an interesting book about one of the lesser known churches of the Restoration.  The first part of the book is a historical sketch written by Mrs. Fletcher, in which she arranged in chronological order the known facts about Mr. Cutler, his family, places where he resided, and his affiliation with The Church of Jesus Christ and the restored gospel.  The balance of the book is a collection of articles on doctrinal beliefs written by Mr. Fletcher, in which he gives the reader an insight into the teachings of the Church, along with it's beliefs on various subjects, and a pattern for future development.

Joseph Smith Fought Polygamy

     This is an excellent reference book that is co-authored by Mr. and Mrs. Richard Price.  It explains how men nearest the prophet attached polygamy to his name in order to justify their own polygamous crimes. 

     I quote from the words of Richard and Pamela as expressed in the preface to the book:    'Over the years our endeavors turned into an exhaustive research project as we gathered information by travel and correspondence from many libraries throughout the United States and England.  In these studies it was discovered that polygamy as it is known among the Mormons did not begin with Joseph, but was brought into the Church by Missionaries and their converts.'
