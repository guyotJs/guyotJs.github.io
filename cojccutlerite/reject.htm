THE REJECTION OF THE CHURCH

     The rejection of the Church is a subject that is neither accepted nor understood by the majority of people in the restoration movement. It came about as a result of the failure of the Church membership to keep a direct commandment given to them from God concerning the building of the Nauvoo Temple.

     Reference: Book of Doctrine & Covenants; 1844-1846 editions, section 103, verses 10 & 11; RLDS edition, section 107, verses 10 & 11; UTAH edition, section 124, verses 25 thru 36.

     I would like to quote 3 sentences from the above references:

"But I command you, all ye my Saints, to build a house unto me; and I grant unto you a sufficient time to build a house unto me, and during this time your baptisms shall be acceptable unto me.

     But behold, at the end of this appointment, your baptisms for your dead shall not be acceptable unto me; and if you do not these things at the end of the appointment, ye shall be rejected as a church with your dead, saith the Lord your God.

     For verily I say unto you, that after you have had sufficient time to build a house to me, wherein the ordinance of baptizing for the dead belongeth, and for which the same was instituted from before the foundation of the world, your baptisms for your dead can not be acceptable unto me; for therein are the keys of the holy priesthood ordained, that you may receive honor and glory."

     From the above, we see that God’s people were given a direct commandment to build this temple, and the consequences were very plainly spelled out if they did not accomplish this in the appointed time, namely being "rejected as a church with their dead". Now we know from historical accounts that, not only was the temple never completed, but it was allowed to be desecrated and destroyed, and the Saint’s homes were ransacked and they were driven out…all because of their failure to keep his commandment. They were not willing to impart of their substance what was required of them, so they were forced to suffer the consequences. It is true they got far enough along with the temple to perform some of the ordinances inside, but the fact remains that it was never completely finished. Therefore the Church was, of necessity, rejected just as he had promised, as God is unchangeable and cannot lie.

     Now the reader might ask "How was this possible when God had told them that his "Kingdom" would never again leave the earth"? Let us examine this statement as set forth in section 85 (section 87 in RLDS, and section 90 in UTAH editions) of the Doctrine & Covenants. Verse 1 says "Therefore art thou blessed from henceforth that bear the keys of the kingdom given unto you; which kingdom is coming forth for the last time." Verse 2 says "Verily I say unto you, the keys of this kingdom shall never be taken from you, while thou art in this world, neither in the world to come; nevertheless, through you shall the oracles be given to another; yea, even unto the Church." And verse 5 says "And this shall be your business and mission in all your lives to preside in council and set in order all the affairs of this Church and Kingdom."

     From the above we begin to understand the distinction made between the "Church" and the "Kingdom". Even though the Church was rejected, the Kingdom authority remained as was promised, never again to leave the earth. Alpheus Cutler was the only leader coming out of the original Church that understood this concept in it’s entirety, and kept it intact.

     Now let us analyze what we have learned so far; We know that God cannot lie and that he keeps all of his promises. We know the temple was never completed, so then the Church must have been rejected just as he promised it would be. This being the case, then the only way this could happen is through the way he provided, with the Church and Kingdom being separate entities. Only 3 groups accepted the fact that the Church had been rejected and realized the need for a reorganization. Of these 3, only the one reorganized by Cutler still adheres to all of the original doctrines that were restored in their purity by Joseph Smith, with no additions or deletions.

     For a more in-depth treatise on this subject, you may read section 6 titled "The Scattered Children Of Zion" in the book "Alpheus Cutler and the Church of Jesus Christ" by Rupert J. Fletcher & Daisy (Whiting) Fletcher. Chapters 7 (Alpheus Cutler And Zenos Gurley Letters) and 8 (Alpheus Cutler On Reorganization) in this section are especially relevant and helpful.
